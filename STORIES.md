## Story 1 — The Old Lavender Farm
**Status:** Will be re-recorded with Aoede voice
**Background:** Soft wind, countryside ambience
**File:** `old-lavender-farm.mp3`

There is a farm at the edge of a valley.

You have been walking for a long time — not with tired legs, but with a tired mind. The kind of tired that builds slowly, without you noticing, until one evening you simply stop.

This is where you stop.

The lavender grows in long rows here. It has grown this way for many years, long before you arrived, and it will continue long after tonight. There is something quietly comforting in that.

The sun has just gone below the hills. The sky is that particular shade of blue that has no name — not quite night, not quite evening. A held breath.

You sit on the low stone wall at the edge of the field. The lavender moves in a slow wave, as though breathing.

You breathe with it.

In the farmhouse behind you, a light is on. Someone is inside, doing something quiet. You don't need to know what. You don't need to go in yet.

You are allowed to simply be here, on this wall, in this light, with this smell.

Lavender has been used for sleep for thousands of years. Not because it forces rest — but because it reminds the body that rest is safe.

Your body already knows how to sleep.

You are just reminding it.

The first stars are appearing.

The lavender is still moving.

Your breathing has already slowed.

Let it.

You are safe here. Nothing is needed from you tonight.

Just this field. Just this breath. Just this quiet.

## Story 2 — The Lighthouse Keeper
**Status:** Recorded ✅
**Voice:** Aoede
**Background:** Ocean waves
**File:** `lighthouse-keeper.mp3`

There is a lighthouse at the edge of a quiet coast.

You have been here before. Or perhaps it only feels that way — the way certain places feel familiar the moment you arrive.

The light turns slowly above you. Once around. Then again. Patient as breathing.

Below, the ocean moves in long, slow waves. Not crashing. Just arriving. The way sleep arrives — without asking.

You are sitting by a small window. The glass is thick and old, slightly warped. Through it, the water looks soft and dark.

A lantern burns low on the table beside you. Warm amber. It asks nothing of you.

The waves come and go… come and go…

You notice your hands are resting in your lap. Heavy. Still.

There is no one else here tonight. No messages. No decisions. Just the turning light and the patient sea.

The sound of water below is steady. It has been steady for a thousand years before tonight. It will be steady long after.

You are part of this moment. Nothing more is needed.

The light turns once more.

And the ocean breathes.

And so do you.

Rest now. You are safe here.

The lighthouse will keep watch…

You don't have to.

## Story 3 — The Mountain Cabin
**Status:** Ready to record
**Voice:** Aoede
**Background:** Soft fire crackling
**File:** `mountain-cabin.mp3`

There is a small cabin high in the mountains.

Snow has been falling since before you arrived. Softly. Without hurry.

Inside, a fire burns low in the hearth. Not roaring. Just present. The way a quiet friend sits beside you without needing to speak.

You are wrapped in something warm. A blanket perhaps. Or just the air itself — the cabin holds heat the way old wood does. Slowly. Deeply.

Outside, the snow continues.

There is no wind tonight. Just silence — the particular silence that only snow can make. As if the whole mountain is resting.

Your breathing has already slowed. You may not have noticed.

The fire shifts. A small sound. Then stillness again.

Nothing is required of you here. No one is waiting. No one needs anything.

The mountain has been standing for ten thousand years.

Tonight it is standing for you.

Let your eyes grow heavy.

Let the warmth do what warmth does.

The snow will still be there in the morning.

But that is the morning.

This… is now.

And now is very, very quiet.

Rest.

## Translation Notes

When translating to other languages:
- Keep sentences short and simple
- Preserve the pauses (... and —)
- Do not change the emotional tone
- Record with same Aoede voice in target language
- Same background sounds apply
